How French Salary Deductions Work

In France, the difference between gross (brut) and net salary can be significant. Employees typically see deductions of around 22-25% for social contributions (cotisations sociales), which fund healthcare, retirement, unemployment insurance, and other social programs.

Net Salary ≈ Gross × 0.775 (non-cadre, approximate) Net Salary ≈ Gross × 0.765 (cadre, slightly more deducted) Common deductions from gross: - CSG/CRDS: ~9.2% - Assurance maladie: ~0.75% - Retraite de base: ~6.9% - Retraite complémentaire: ~3.15% (non-cadre) / ~5% (cadre) - Chômage: ~0.4% Total employee charges: ~22-25%
Example — €2,500 gross / month (non-cadre)

Social charges ≈ 22% → €2,500 × 0.22 = €550 deducted

Net salary ≈ €2,500 − €550 = €1,950 net

Note: income tax (impôt sur le revenu) is collected separately via prélèvement à la source.

Frequently Asked Questions

The French SMIC (Salaire Minimum Interprofessionnel de Croissance) is reviewed annually. As of January 2026, the gross SMIC is approximately €1,800/month (35h/week). Net after deductions is approximately €1,400/month. Always verify the current rate on the official government website.
Cadre (executive/manager) status comes with slightly different social contribution rates, higher retirement contributions (AGIRC), and different rights. Cadres typically have a higher gross-to-net ratio due to slightly higher deductions, but also benefit from better retirement plans and unemployment benefits.
