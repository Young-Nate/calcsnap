How Monthly Loan Payments Are Calculated

Banks use the standard amortization formula to calculate fixed monthly payments. Each payment covers the accrued monthly interest plus a portion of the principal. Early payments are mostly interest; later payments are mostly principal.

M = P × [r(1+r)^n] / [(1+r)^n - 1] M = Monthly payment P = Loan principal (amount borrowed) r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12) n = Number of monthly payments (years × 12)
Example — €150,000 mortgage at 3.5% for 20 years

Monthly rate r = 3.5% / 12 = 0.2917% = 0.002917

n = 20 × 12 = 240 payments

M = 150,000 × [0.002917 × (1.002917)^240] / [(1.002917)^240 − 1]

M ≈ €869.84/month

Total paid = 869.84 × 240 = €208,762 (€58,762 in interest)

Frequently Asked Questions

French mortgage rates fluctuate with the ECB base rate. In early 2026, rates for 20-year loans are typically between 3.0% and 4.5% depending on the bank, your profile, and loan-to-value ratio. Use this calculator with your actual quoted rate from a bank for the most accurate estimate.
The HCSF (Haut Conseil de Stabilité Financière) limits total debt repayments to 35% of gross income in France. This includes your new mortgage plus any existing loans. If your monthly income is €4,000, maximum total repayments should not exceed €1,400/month.
A shorter term means higher monthly payments but much less interest paid overall. A longer term reduces monthly payments but significantly increases total interest. Use this calculator to compare: try the same loan at 15, 20, and 25 years to see the total interest difference — it can be tens of thousands of euros.
