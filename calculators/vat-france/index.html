French VAT Rates — Guide

France (TVA — Taxe sur la Valeur Ajoutée) has four VAT rates. Most goods and services use the standard rate of 20%. Understanding which rate applies is essential for businesses and consumers.

Rate Category Examples
20% Standard (Taux normal) Electronics, clothing, cars, most services
10% Intermediate (Taux intermédiaire) Restaurant meals, agricultural products, transport, renovation
5.5% Reduced (Taux réduit) Food, books, energy, accessibility equipment
2.1% Super-reduced (Taux super-réduit) Press, medication (prescription), TV licence
Add VAT: TTC = HT × (1 + TVA_rate / 100) Remove VAT: HT = TTC / (1 + TVA_rate / 100) VAT Amount: TVA = TTC − HT
Example — Adding 20% TVA to €500 HT

TTC = 500 × (1 + 0.20) = 500 × 1.20 = €600 TTC

TVA amount = 600 − 500 = €100

Example — Removing 20% TVA from €120 TTC

HT = 120 / 1.20 = €100 HT

TVA amount = 120 − 100 = €20

Frequently Asked Questions

HT (Hors Taxes) means the price before tax — the net price. TTC (Toutes Taxes Comprises) means the price including all taxes. Businesses usually work in HT; consumers pay TTC. Invoices to other businesses show both.
Restaurant meals consumed on-site are taxed at 10% (reduced rate). Take-away food is taxed at 5.5% if it's the type you normally eat cold (e.g. sandwiches), or 10% if it's hot food. Alcohol is always taxed at 20%.
