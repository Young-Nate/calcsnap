UK Income Tax & NI Rates 2025/26

The UK uses a Pay As You Earn (PAYE) system. Your employer deducts Income Tax and National Insurance (NI) from each payslip before you're paid.

Personal Allowance: £12,570 (tax-free) Basic Rate (20%): £12,571 – £50,270 Higher Rate (40%): £50,271 – £125,140 Additional Rate (45%): over £125,140 National Insurance (Class 1, employee): 8% on earnings £12,570 – £50,270/year 2% on earnings above £50,270/year
Example — £35,000 gross annual (tax code 1257L)

Taxable income = £35,000 − £12,570 = £22,430

Income Tax = £22,430 × 20% = £4,486/year

NI = (£35,000 − £12,570) × 8% = £1,794/year

Net annual = £35,000 − £4,486 − £1,794 = £28,720 (~£2,393/month)
