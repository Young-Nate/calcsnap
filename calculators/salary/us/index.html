How US Federal Income Tax Works

The US uses a progressive tax bracket system — you pay each bracket's rate only on income within that range, not your entire salary. On top of federal income tax, most employees also pay FICA taxes: Social Security (6.2% up to $168,600) and Medicare (1.45% on all wages).

2025 Federal Tax Brackets (Single): 10% on income $0 – $11,600 12% on income $11,601 – $47,150 22% on income $47,151 – $100,525 24% on income $100,526 – $191,950 32% on income $191,951 – $243,725 35% on income $243,726 – $609,350 37% on income above $609,350 Standard deduction 2025: $14,600 (single) / $29,200 (married) Social Security: 6.2% on wages up to $168,600 Medicare: 1.45% on all wages (+0.9% above $200,000)
Example — $75,000 gross salary (Single, 2025)

Taxable income = $75,000 − $14,600 = $60,400

Federal tax = 10% of $11,600 + 12% of $35,550 + 22% of $13,250 = ~$10,294

Social Security = $75,000 × 6.2% = $4,650

Medicare = $75,000 × 1.45% = $1,088

Net annual = $75,000 − $10,294 − $4,650 − $1,088 = ~$58,968 (~$4,914/mo)
