How Canadian Federal Tax Works

Canada uses a progressive federal income tax system. After claiming the Basic Personal Amount (BPA) of $15,705, the remaining income is taxed at graduated rates. Provincial tax is added on top — each province sets its own brackets. CPP (Canada Pension Plan) and EI (Employment Insurance) are mandatory payroll deductions.

2025 Federal Tax Brackets: 15% on income $0 – $57,375 20.5% on income $57,376 – $114,750 26% on income $114,751 – $158,519 29% on income $158,520 – $220,000 33% on income above $220,000 Basic Personal Amount: $15,705 (federal credit) CPP: 5.95% on earnings $3,500 – $68,500 (max $3,867) EI: 1.66% on insurable earnings up to $63,200 (max $1,049)
Example — $80,000 gross salary (Ontario, 2025)

Federal taxable income ≈ $80,000 (after BPA non-refundable credit applied)

Federal tax ≈ $13,478 (before BPA credit of $2,356) = ~$11,122

Ontario provincial tax ≈ $80,000 × 5.5% = ~$4,400

CPP = ($68,500 − $3,500) × 5.95% = $3,867

EI = $63,200 × 1.66% = $1,049

Net annual ≈ $80,000 − $11,122 − $4,400 − $3,867 − $1,049 = ~$59,562 (~$4,964/mo)

Frequently Asked Questions

The federal Basic Personal Amount (BPA) for 2025 is $15,705. This is a non-refundable tax credit — it reduces your federal tax bill by $15,705 × 15% = $2,356, rather than reducing your taxable income directly. Most provinces also have their own basic personal amount.
The Canada Pension Plan (CPP) is a mandatory retirement savings program. Employees contribute 5.95% on earnings between $3,500 (exemption) and $68,500 (maximum pensionable earnings), for a maximum annual contribution of $3,867 in 2025. Employers match this amount. Quebec residents contribute to QPP instead of CPP.
Alberta has the lowest provincial income tax in Canada, with a flat rate starting at 10% (and no provincial sales tax). Ontario and British Columbia have relatively moderate rates for most income levels, while Quebec and Nova Scotia tend to have higher combined rates. The province you live and work in determines which provincial rates apply.
