Margin vs. Markup — What's the Difference?

Margin and markup both describe profitability, but they use different bases for calculation. Confusing them is one of the most common business pricing mistakes.

Gross Profit = Revenue − Cost Margin = (Profit / Revenue) × 100 ← based on revenue Markup = (Profit / Cost) × 100 ← based on cost Selling Price from Margin: Revenue = Cost / (1 − Margin%) Selling Price from Markup: Revenue = Cost × (1 + Markup%)
Example — Cost €60, Selling Price €100

Gross Profit = 100 − 60 = €40

Margin = (40 / 100) × 100 = 40%

Markup = (40 / 60) × 100 = 66.7%

A 40% margin is NOT the same as a 40% markup. The margin is always lower than the markup for the same product.

Frequently Asked Questions

It depends heavily on the industry. Software/SaaS: 60-90% gross margin. Retail: 20-50%. Restaurants: 3-9%. Professional services: 25-50%. Manufacturing: 25-40%. Always compare within your industry. A 5% margin in retail is normal; in software it would signal serious problems.
Use the formula: Selling Price = Cost ÷ (1 − Desired Margin%). For a 40% margin on a €60 cost item: Price = 60 ÷ 0.60 = €100. Use Mode 2 (From Margin %) in this calculator to find the selling price automatically.
