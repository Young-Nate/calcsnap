US Sales Tax — How It Works

Unlike VAT in Europe, US sales tax is only charged at the point of sale to the end consumer — businesses do not pay it on purchases for resale. Rates vary by state and can also vary by county and city, making the combined rate higher than the state rate alone.

Add tax: Total = Pre-tax price × (1 + rate / 100) Remove tax: Pre-tax = Total / (1 + rate / 100) Tax amount: Tax = Total − Pre-tax Note: unlike EU VAT, US sales tax is NOT included in displayed prices. The tax is added at checkout.
Example — $89.99 item in California (~8.68%)

Tax = $89.99 × 0.0868 = $7.81

Total at checkout = $89.99 + $7.81 = $97.80

States with no sales tax

Alaska, Delaware, Montana, New Hampshire, Oregon have no statewide sales tax. Alaska allows local sales taxes, so the combined rate may still be above 0%.
