UK VAT Rates Explained

The United Kingdom has three VAT rates set by HMRC. Most businesses with taxable turnover above £90,000 (2024/25 threshold) must register for VAT.

Rate Category Examples
20%Standard RateElectronics, clothing, cars, most services
5%Reduced RateHome energy, children's car seats, sanitary products
0%Zero RateMost food, children's clothes, books, newspapers
Add VAT: Gross = Net × (1 + VAT rate / 100) Remove VAT: Net = Gross / (1 + VAT rate / 100) VAT amount: VAT = Gross − Net
Example — Adding 20% VAT to £500 net

Gross = 500 × 1.20 = £600 gross

VAT amount = 600 − 500 = £100

Example — Removing 20% VAT from £120 gross

Net = 120 / 1.20 = £100 net

VAT = 120 − 100 = £20

Frequently Asked Questions

For the 2024/25 tax year, businesses must register for VAT when their taxable turnover exceeds £90,000 in a rolling 12-month period. Once registered, you must charge VAT on taxable sales and can reclaim VAT on purchases.
Zero-rated supplies are taxable at 0% — businesses still charge VAT (at 0%) and can reclaim input VAT. Exempt supplies are outside the VAT system entirely — businesses cannot charge VAT and cannot reclaim input VAT on related costs. Examples of exempt supplies include insurance and financial services.
VAT-registered businesses complete a VAT Return (usually quarterly) via HMRC's Making Tax Digital (MTD) system. If your input VAT (paid on purchases) exceeds your output VAT (charged to customers), HMRC will refund the difference.
