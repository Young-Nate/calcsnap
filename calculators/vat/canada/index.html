Canadian Sales Tax — GST, HST, PST Explained

Canada has a dual system: a federal Goods and Services Tax (GST) at 5%, plus provincial taxes that vary by province. Some provinces have merged their provincial tax with the GST into a single Harmonized Sales Tax (HST).

Add tax: Total = Pre-tax × (1 + rate / 100) Remove tax: Pre-tax = Total / (1 + rate / 100) Tax amount: Tax = Total − Pre-tax
Example — $200 pre-tax in Ontario (13% HST)

HST = $200 × 0.13 = $26.00

Total = $200 + $26 = $226.00

Frequently Asked Questions

GST (Goods and Services Tax) is the federal 5% tax collected by the CRA. PST (Provincial Sales Tax) is a separate provincial tax collected alongside GST. HST (Harmonized Sales Tax) is a combined federal+provincial tax used in Ontario, the Atlantic provinces, and PEI — one rate, one remittance.
Alberta, Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon have no provincial sales tax — only the 5% federal GST applies. This makes Alberta the most tax-friendly province for shopping with a total rate of just 5%.
Businesses must register for GST/HST once their taxable revenue exceeds CAD $30,000 in a single calendar quarter or in four consecutive quarters. Businesses below this threshold are considered "small suppliers" and registration is optional.
