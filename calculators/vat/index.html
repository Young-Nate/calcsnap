Frequently Asked Questions

VAT (Value Added Tax) is a consumption tax applied at each stage of production and distribution. Businesses collect VAT from customers and pay it to the tax authority, minus the VAT they paid on their own purchases. The end consumer bears the full tax. The US uses a different system called sales tax, applied only at the point of final sale.
Hungary has the highest standard VAT rate in the EU at 27%. Among the countries covered here, Portugal leads at 23%, followed by Italy at 22%, then Spain, Belgium, and the Netherlands at 21%.
